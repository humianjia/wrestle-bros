These standards describe how Wrestle Bros Space keeps landing pages readable, differentiated, and accountable at the site level.

Core Editorial Goals

  • Provide enough written context so a visitor can understand a page before launching the game.
  • Separate game categories clearly so visitors are not forced through generic template pages.
  • Maintain reachable support, legal, and privacy information across the site.
  • Reduce repeated filler language and strengthen page-level clarity over time.

What We Expect From Game Pages

  • A unique title and canonical URL.
  • A readable description that explains the game's style or pace.
  • Category placement that matches the page theme.
  • Links back to core site pages and support information.
  • Enough original page-level context to justify why the title is part of the featured catalog.

What We Avoid

  • Blank embed-only screens with no explanation or navigation.
  • Misleading page titles or unrelated metadata.
  • Broken branding or inconsistent legal/support links.
  • Long blocks of repetitive template text that add no page-specific value.

How We Improve Existing Pages

We periodically revise older landing pages to replace thin descriptions with more useful summaries, audience guidance, and quick play tips. We also correct category labels, metadata, footer links, and residual template artifacts when they are found. Pages that have not yet been refreshed may stay accessible by direct URL while being removed from featured navigation until they are stronger.

Site-Level Trust Signals

  • Root-level ads.txt support.
  • Public privacy, cookie, terms, DMCA, and contact pages.
  • Indexable HTML pages and a maintained sitemap.
  • A public disclosure page explaining the role of third-party embeds.
  • Reachable support email for corrections and rights issues.

Questions Or Corrections

If you believe a page description is inaccurate, a link is broken, or a category assignment should be reconsidered, contact us through the contact page.
