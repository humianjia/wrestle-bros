Wrestle Bros Space uses a lightweight review process to improve how each browser game page is described, placed, and linked inside the site.

What We Look At First

  • Whether the page title and metadata match the actual game.
  • Whether the game belongs in the category where it is listed.
  • Whether the page gives visitors enough context before they play.
  • Whether support, legal, and privacy paths are still reachable from that page.

How We Describe Games

We aim to summarize what makes a page useful in practice: is it fast or tactical, casual or competitive, replayable or progression-based, solo-friendly or better for repeated multiplayer sessions. The goal is not to write a full review, but to give a clearer reason to click.

What Gets Updated During Review

  • Meta descriptions and social-preview summaries.
  • Short page intros and gameplay framing.
  • Category labels and related-game section titles.
  • Footer links, branding, and residual template text.

How We Handle Older Pages

Older pages may start with thinner template descriptions. When those pages are reviewed, we expand them with audience guidance, quick tips, and clearer landing-page context so they feel more distinct and more useful to browse.

What This Process Is Not

This site-level review process does not guarantee a score, ranking, or endorsement of any specific game. It is a content-quality pass designed to make the catalog easier to browse and easier to understand.

Reporting Issues

If a page seems mislabeled, incomplete, or broken, you can report it through the contact page so it can be reviewed again.
